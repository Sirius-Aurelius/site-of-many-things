# Description
>*The room is a tiered lecture hall, with rickety wooden desks arranged in a semi-circle, each one scarred with the initials of generations of students and the scorch marks of failed spells. The air in the charms classroom hums with the magical residue of all those previously levitated feathers and whispered incantations. It is a place of gentle chaos and hidden power, where dust motes dance in the sunlight that streams through the high, arched windows.*
>*From the high ceiling hangs a dazzling collection of curious objects: a flock of enchanted paper birds perpetually soaring in lazy circles, a grandfather clock whose hands spin in a dizzying frenzy, and an ornate, silver candelabra whose flames flicker with a life of their own. The walls are lined with shelves packed with ancient, leather-bound books and glass cases showcasing a bizarre collection of magical artifacts—a self-stirring cauldron, a set of invisible inkwells, and a delicate, floating hourglass that measures time in a trickle of starlight. The room smells of old paper, beeswax, and the faint, coppery tang of magic, a scent that your parents would remember as being as much a part of the school as the stone corridors or the whispering portraits.*
